Vocal Variety & Body Language

Your voice and body are your most powerful instruments. Today you learn to use them like a pro!

60 Minutes Grades 4-6 Your Instrument

Physical & Vocal Warm-Up

5 Minutes

The "Big Stretch"

Reach your arms high above your head, then hang low and touch your toes. Loosen the instrument — your body!

The "Accordion" Breath

Put your hands on your ribs. Breathe in deeply and feel your ribs expand outward like an accordion. This deep rib-expansion breath powers your voice!

The Vocal Siren

Slide your voice from your lowest note alllll the way to your highest, like a fire truck siren. Wheeee-ooooo! This stretches your vocal range.

Raspberries & Lip Trills

Brrrrrrr! Blow raspberries to loosen up your lips and get the vibrations going. Then try humming while you do it.

Why Warm Up?

Athletes stretch before a game. Singers warm up before a concert. Your voice and body are your instruments — treat them that way!

Vocal Variety

15 Minutes

The Whisper (Level 1)

When do you whisper? Secrets, scary moments, surprises.

Whispering actually makes the audience lean in closer to listen. It pulls them into your world.

Try it:

"I opened the door slowly... and what I saw... changed everything."

The Megaphone (Level 5)

When do you yell? Excitement, anger, cheering!

Use this when you reach the "Boss Battle" or the climax of your story. It wakes the audience up!

Try it:

"And the crowd went WILD!"

The Speed Pedal (Pace)

  • The Cheetah (Fast): Use for action! "I grabbed the controller, I hit the button, the screen flashed, and BOOM!" Fast speech makes the audience's heart beat faster.
  • The Turtle (Slow): Use for serious moments, or right before a big reveal. "I opened the door... and there it was." Pausing and speaking slowly builds suspense.

The Character Switch (Voices)

You are the narrator of your speech. But sometimes, other characters talk! Change your posture and voice to play a different character for just one sentence.

Example:

"My mom put her hands on her hips and said, [strict adult voice] 'Clean your room!'"

"A great speaker doesn't just tell you the story. They make you FEEL it — through volume, speed, and character!"

Body Language

15 Minutes

Base Camp (The Resting Position)

When you aren't doing anything in a game, your character stands in their "idle animation." Your idle animation is Base Camp.

  • Feet shoulder-width apart
  • Hands drop totally relaxed to the side
  • No pockets. No crossing arms. No wringing hands.

The Rule:

"If you don't know what to do with your hands, send them to Base Camp."

The 3 Tool Gestures

Your hands are tools to help the audience see the words!

The Number Tool

If you say "I have three reasons," you must hold up 3 fingers!

The Size Tool

If something is tiny, pinch your fingers. If it is massive, stretch your arms as wide as they go!

The Heart Tool

If you are talking about yourself, your feelings, or your opinion, bring one hand to your chest.

Stage Movement: "Walk and Plant"

The Mistake: "The Tiger in the Cage" — pacing back and forth nervously without stopping.

The Fix: "Walk on a period." Stand in the center for your first point. When you transition to your next point, take three steps to the left, plant your feet like tree roots, and stay there.

The Golden Rule:

Never speak while your feet are moving aimlessly.

"Your hands and face speak just as loudly as your voice. Make every movement intentional!"

Exercise: The Silent Storyteller

10 Minutes
The Challenge

Act out a scenario for 30 seconds without making a single sound. The group guesses what you're doing!

"You are trying to walk a very tiny, energetic dog."

"You just walked into a room that smells like old socks."

"You are a scientist who just discovered a new species of bug."

"You are trying to build a Lego set, but the instructions are in a different language."

Discussion After Each Turn

"What did they do with their hands or face that helped you understand the story?"

Impromptu Speaking

10 Minutes
Quick Cheat Sheet

Pick the right framework for the right question!

OREO

Use for: Opinion questions
  • O Opinion - State your opinion
  • R Reason - Give your reason
  • E Example - Share an example
  • O Opinion - Repeat your opinion

Time Traveler

Use for: Past/Present/Future questions
  • P Past - How things used to be
  • P Present - How things are now
  • F Future - How things could be

Sandwich

Use for: "Tell us about..." topics
  • T Top Bun - Introduce the topic
  • M Meat - 2-3 details or reasons
  • B Bottom Bun - Wrap it up

Practice Prompts

  • OREO: "What is the best holiday and why?"
  • OREO: "Should kids have homework on weekends?"
  • Time Traveler: "How has the way we play games changed over time?"
  • Time Traveler: "How has the way we communicate changed?"
  • Sandwich: "Tell us about your favorite place to visit."
  • Sandwich: "Tell us about a skill you're proud of."

Closing: The Mirror Challenge

5 Minutes

How to Play

Pair up! One person makes a facial expression or gesture — Confident, Thinking, or Nervous — and the other must mirror it perfectly.

Your face and hands "speak" just as loudly as your voice. Make every movement count!

"1, 2, 3... USE YOUR BODY!"

Homework Challenge

"This week, watch a speech, a movie scene, or a commercial with the sound OFF. What can you understand just from body language? Write down 3 things you noticed and bring it to next class!"
