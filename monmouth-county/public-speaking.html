It’s About People, Not Words

Public speaking isn’t a performance. It’s a conversation at scale. The goal isn’t to be impressive — it’s to be understood.

See Them

Eye contact — not the back wall, not your slides. Actual humans.

Speak To Them

Use “you” and “we.” Pretend each row is a friend at coffee.

Care About Them

Audiences feel intent. If you care, they’ll lean in.

Taming the Butterflies

Nerves are not your enemy — they’re your body saying “this matters.” Channel them.

1

Box Breathe

4 in · 4 hold · 4 out · 4 hold. Twice before you walk on.

2

Power Pose

Arms wide, chest open, 60 seconds backstage. Real science.

3

First 30 Seconds

Memorize cold. Once you survive the opener, momentum carries you.

4

Reframe

Don’t say “I’m nervous.” Say “I’m excited.” Same chemicals, better story.

The Power of Your Story

“Facts tell. Stories sell. And stories are what we remember.

Without a Story

“Our department processed 14,000 cases last year, a 12% increase.”

…forgotten by lunch.

With a Story

“Mrs. Thompson called us in tears. Sixty seconds later we had her answer. We did that 14,000 times this year.”

…remembered for years.

Body Language Basics

Your audience reads your body before they hear your words. Make the message match.

Plant Your Feet

Hip-width apart. No swaying, no pacing. Stillness = authority.

Open Your Hands

Palms visible, gestures from the elbow. Closed fists feel defensive.

Soft Eyes, Real Smile

Lock eyes for 3 seconds at a time. Smile like you mean it — the room mirrors you.

Group Exercise

The Human Commercial

Turn to a partner. Learn one fun fact about them in 60 seconds.

Then stand up and spend 30 seconds “pitching” them to the table — like a commercial.

5-Minute Activity
Energy > Polish Use Their Name Make Them Look Good

Visuals That Work

The slide is the backup singer. You are the lead.

Avoid

  • Walls of text
  • Reading your slides aloud
  • 8-color charts no one can decode
  • Tiny fonts — row 12 can’t see them

Embrace

  • One big idea per slide
  • A single bold image or stat
  • Bullets ≤ 6 words
  • White space — let it breathe

Reading a Room of 70+ People

A big audience isn’t one giant face — it’s clusters of small ones.

1

Zone the Room

Mentally split into left / center / right. Speak to each zone every minute.

2

Ask & Pause

“Show of hands — who’s seen this before?” Pulls attention back instantly.

3

Watch the Phones

If 3+ phones come up, change something — pace, volume, or topic.
