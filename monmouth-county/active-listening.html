Hearing vs. Listening

One is automatic. One is a choice — and it changes everything.

Hearing

Sound waves hitting your ears.

Passive. Effortless. Background noise.

Result: You were physically present.

Listening

Choosing to focus, decode, and respond.

Active. Effortful. Eyes & mind on the speaker.

Result: The other person felt seen.

Common Roadblocks

If you spot these in yourself, you’re halfway to fixing them.

Rehearsing

Crafting your reply while they’re still talking. You stop hearing them at word 3.

Distractions

Phone face-up. Email pings. Open laptop. The signal: you don’t matter.

Filtering

Only hearing what you agree with — or what confirms your decision.

Reading the Unspoken

Words are 7% of the message. The other 93%? Tone, face, body.


Eyes

Looking down? Anxious. Looking away? Disagreeing.


Hands

Crossed arms = guarded. Open palms = inviting.


Tone

Same words, different pitch — opposite meaning.


Pauses

A long silence often means “there’s more here.”

The Mirror Technique

Confirm before you respond. It saves hours of rework later.

1

Paraphrase

“So what I’m hearing is…”

2

Reflect Feelings

“It sounds like that was frustrating.”

3

Confirm

“Did I get that right?”

“People don’t want advice. They want to feel understood first.
Group Exercise

The Telephone Improv

Tables of 5+. Person 1 starts a story with one sentence.

Each person continues with “Yes, and…” — no “but,” no “however.”

You can only build on what you actually heard.

7-Minute Activity
Yes, and… No interrupting Have fun with it

Empathy in Action

Step into their shoes before you offer your fix. The frustrated citizen on line 1 just wants to be heard.

Without Empathy

“That’s not our department. You’ll need to call…”

Caller: shouts louder.

With Empathy

“That sounds incredibly stressful — and I want to make sure you reach the right person on the first try.”

Caller: exhales.

The Power of the Pause

Silence is not awkward — it’s where real thinking happens. Most people quit waiting too soon.

“Wait 3 full seconds after they finish. The most honest words come after the pause.

They Process

The brain needs a beat to find the real answer.

They Add

The most useful detail usually comes after the silence.

You Calm Down

You stop reacting and start responding.

Better Meetings

Active listening turns 60-minute meetings into 30-minute decisions.

1

One Voice at a Time

No talk-overs. The cost of one re-explanation? 5 minutes × 8 people.

2

Recap Before You Move

30 seconds at the end of each topic: “So we agreed…”

3

Phones Down

Lid down on laptops. Eyes up. Stress drops, decisions speed up.

30% shorter Better follow-through Less burnout
