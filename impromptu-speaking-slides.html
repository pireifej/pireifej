Session 2

Impromptu Speaking

Master the "cheat codes" of speaking on the spot. Trust your brain, grab a framework, and speak with confidence!

60 Minutes Grades 4-6 3 Cheat Codes

Warm-Up: "Brain Ping-Pong"

10 Minutes
The Goal

Wake up the brain and stop overthinking. Speed is key!

The Game

Setup: Stand in a circle.

  • The Teacher starts by throwing an imaginary ball (or a real foam ball) to a student and saying ONE word (e.g., "Spatula")
  • The student catches it and immediately throws it to someone else saying the first word that connects to it (e.g., "Pancakes")
  • Next student: "Syrup." Next: "Sticky." Next: "Glue."
  • Keep it going as fast as possible!

"If you paused to think of the 'perfect' word, the game slowed down. Impromptu speaking is about trusting your first thought!"

The "Cheat Codes" (3 Frameworks)

15 Minutes
The Big Idea

"When a video game level is too hard, sometimes you use a Cheat Code. In speaking, we use Frameworks. If I ask you a question, don't panic. Just pick a code."

Cheat Code #1: O.R.E.O.

Best for "Opinion" Questions like "Do you like X?" or "What is your favorite...?"

Cheat Code #2: Time Traveler

Best for "Tell us about..." Questions - great for big topics or history.

Cheat Code #3: Story Sandwich

Best for "What would you do?" Questions - hypothetical situations.

Remember

You don't need to write a speech - you just need a structure to hang your words on!

Cheat Code #1: The O.R.E.O.

Opinion Questions

The O.R.E.O. Framework

Use this when asked: "Do you like X?" or "What is your favorite...?"

  • OOpinion: State clearly how you feel. "I think Minecraft is the best game ever."
  • RReason: Why? "Because you can build anything you can imagine."
  • EExample: Give a specific detail. "Once, I built a castle that went all the way to the clouds."
  • OOpinion: Restate it. "That is why Minecraft is the king of games."

Opinion + Reason + Example + Opinion = A complete answer every time!

Cheat Code #2: The Time Traveler

"Tell us about..." Questions

The Framework

Use this for big topics or history - travel through time!

  • PAST: How was it before?
  • PRESENT: How is it now?
  • FUTURE: What do you hope happens?

Example: "Tell us about how we listen to music."

PAST: "In the past, my parents told me they had to carry around giant boomboxes or CD players that skipped every time they walked. They could only carry like 12 songs at a time!"

PRESENT: "Today, I have Spotify on my phone. I have 10 million songs in my pocket and I can listen to anything I want, anytime, instantly."

FUTURE: "In the future, I bet we won't even need headphones. The music will just be beamed directly to a chip in our ear, or maybe walls will play music automatically when we walk into a room."

Example: "Tell us about your time at this school."

PAST: "When I started here in Kindergarten, I was terrified. The hallways felt huge, I couldn't tie my shoes, and my biggest worry was nap time."

PRESENT: "Now that I am in 5th grade, I know where every classroom is. I play soccer at recess, and I'm learning complex math."

FUTURE: "In the future, when I graduate from here, I hope to leave a legacy as the best soccer goalie this school has ever seen."

Cheat Code #3: The Story Sandwich

"What would you do?" Questions

The Framework

Use this for hypothetical situations - imagine anything!

  • TOP BUN (The Hook): Start with a bold statement or sound effect. "BOOM! That's the sound of me winning the lottery."
  • THE MEAT (The Story): Tell the story of what you would do. "First, I'd buy a house made of candy. Then I'd buy a jetpack..."
  • BOTTOM BUN (The Lesson): Why does this matter? "But seriously, I would mostly help my family."

Hook them, tell them, teach them!

Group Drill: "The Skeleton Run"

10 Minutes
The Goal

Practice OUTLINING only. Do not give the full speech. This builds the muscle of organizing thoughts quickly.

The Skeleton (3 Bones)

Instruction: "I am going to ask a question. You are NOT going to give a speech. You are only going to give me the Skeleton (The 3 Bones) of your answer."

Example Question: "What is the best pet?"

Student Response: "Dog. Bone 1: They cuddle. Bone 2: They protect the house. Bone 3: They play fetch."

Practice Topics

Go around the room fast! Answer + 3 Points only.

  • What is the best school lunch?
  • Cats vs. Dogs: Who wins?
  • What is the worst chore to do at home?
  • Is it better to be a Ninja or a Pirate?
  • What is the one food you would eat for the rest of your life?
  • Summer Vacation vs. Winter Break?
  • If you could have any superpower, what would it be?
  • What is the most annoying noise in the world?
  • Video Games vs. Playing Outside?
  • What is the best candy in the Halloween bag?

The Boss Battle: Random Encounters

20 Minutes

The Rules of Battle

  • Take a Breath: Remember the "Fear Monster" from last week - breathe to tame it
  • Pick a Cheat Code: O.R.E.O., Time Traveler, or Story Sandwich
  • Speak for 1 minute using your chosen framework

10 Impromptu Questions

1If you could have any superpower, but only for 24 hours, what would it be and what would you do?
Target: Story Sandwich
2If you were the Principal of this school for one day, what two rules would you change?
Target: O.R.E.O
3Which is better: Being able to fly or being able to turn invisible?
Target: O.R.E.O
4If you could live inside any video game or movie, which one would you pick?
Target: Story Sandwich
5Is it better to have Summer all year long or Winter all year long?
Target: O.R.E.O
6Tell us about a time you tried something new and it didn't go as planned.
Target: Time Traveler
7If animals could talk, which animal would be the rudest and why?
Target: Creative/Story
8Should kids get paid money for doing chores, or should they do them for free?
Target: O.R.E.O
9If you found a magic door in your bedroom, where would it lead?
Target: Story Sandwich
10What do you think cars and transportation will look like in 50 years?
Target: Time Traveler

Cooldown & Review

5 Minutes

Quick Review

"Who can tell me what O.R.E.O. stands for?"

  • O - Opinion
  • R - Reason
  • E - Example
  • O - Opinion (restate)

Homework Challenge

"Next time you are at the dinner table and someone asks 'How was school?', don't just say 'Good.' Use the Time Traveler code: 'This morning was tired, Lunch was pizza, and Tomorrow I have a test!'"
