Brain Ping-Pong Warm-Up

The Game

Stand in a circle. Someone throws an imaginary ball and says ONE word (like "Spatula"). You catch it and immediately say the first connected word that pops into your head ("Pancakes"). Keep it going fast!

The big lesson: If you pause to think of the "perfect" word, the game slows down. Impromptu speaking is about trusting your FIRST thought!

The 3 "Cheat Codes"

What Are Cheat Codes?

When a video game level is too hard, sometimes you use a Cheat Code. In speaking, we use Frameworks. If someone asks you a question, don't panic - just pick a code!

You don't need to write a speech - you just need a structure to hang your words on!

The 3 Frameworks

  • O.R.E.O. - Best for "Opinion" questions like "Do you like X?" or "What is your favorite...?"
  • Time Traveler - Best for "Tell us about..." questions - great for big topics or history
  • Story Sandwich - Best for "What would you do?" questions - hypothetical situations

Cheat Code #1: O.R.E.O.

The O.R.E.O. Framework

Use this when asked opinion questions like "Do you like X?" or "What is your favorite...?"

  • O Opinion: State clearly how you feel. "I think Minecraft is the best game ever."
  • R Reason: Why? "Because you can build anything you can imagine."
  • E Example: Give a specific detail. "Once, I built a castle that went all the way to the clouds."
  • O Opinion: Restate it. "That is why Minecraft is the king of games."

Cheat Codes #2 & #3

The Time Traveler

Use this for "Tell us about..." questions - travel through time!

  • PAST: How was it before?
  • PRESENT: How is it now?
  • FUTURE: What do you hope happens?

Example: "Tell us about how we listen to music."

PAST: "My parents carried around giant boomboxes. They could only carry 12 songs!"

PRESENT: "Today, I have Spotify. 10 million songs in my pocket."

FUTURE: "In the future, music will be beamed directly to a chip in our ear!"

The Story Sandwich

Use this for "What would you do?" questions - hypothetical situations!

  • TOP BUN (The Hook): Start with a bold statement or sound effect
  • THE MEAT (The Story): Tell the story of what you would do
  • BOTTOM BUN (The Lesson): Why does this matter?

The Skeleton Run

What Is It?

In the Skeleton Run, you DON'T give a full speech. You only give the skeleton - your answer plus 3 quick points (the "3 bones"). This builds the muscle of organizing your thoughts quickly.

Example: "What is the best pet?" → "Dog. Bone 1: They cuddle. Bone 2: They protect the house. Bone 3: They play fetch."

Try It at Home!

Ask a family member to give you a random topic. Give your answer + 3 bones as fast as you can. Try these: "Best school lunch?" "Cats vs Dogs?" "Best superpower?"

Review & Remember

Key Takeaways

  • Trust your first thought - don't overthink it, just speak!
  • O.R.E.O. = Opinion, Reason, Example, Opinion (for "favorite" and opinion questions)
  • Time Traveler = Past, Present, Future (for "tell us about" questions)
  • Story Sandwich = Hook, Story, Lesson (for "what would you do" questions)
  • The Skeleton Run = Answer + 3 Bones (practice organizing fast)

Your Homework Challenge

Next time someone at the dinner table asks "How was school?", don't just say "Good." Use the Time Traveler code: "This morning was tired, Lunch was pizza, and Tomorrow I have a test!"
