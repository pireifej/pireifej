Stretches & Warm-Up

5 Minutes

Body Stretches

  • Interlace your fingers and stretch your arms up high above your head
  • Massage your jaw - loosen it up!
  • Shake your right arm, shake your left arm, shake your right foot, shake your left foot
  • Do raspberries! (Brrrrrrr with your lips)

Vocal Warm-Up & Breathing

  • Box Breathing: Inhale for 4 counts, Hold for 4 counts, Exhale for 4 counts
  • Do mmmmmmmm - feel the vibration in your chest
  • Mama papa mama papa - say it fast and get those lips moving!

Why Warm Up?

Athletes stretch before a game. Speakers warm up before a speech. Your voice and body are your instruments!

Part 1: The Introduction - Hooks

15 Minutes
What is a Hook?

A hook is the very first thing you say to GRAB your audience's attention. It makes them think: "Ooh, I want to hear more!"

Hook Type 1: Question

Ask the audience something that makes them think or raise their hands.

Example:

"Have you ever been so scared that your knees started shaking?"

Hook Type 2: Amazing Fact

Share a surprising fact that makes the audience say "Whoa!"

Example:

"Did you know that a human brain can store more information than every library in the world combined?"

Hook Type 3: Story

Start with a short, exciting moment that pulls people in.

Example:

"I was standing on the edge of the diving board, looking down at the water, and my legs wouldn't move."

Part 2: Body & Transitions

15 Minutes
The Rule of Three

Great speeches have THREE main points. Not one, not five - THREE. It's the magic number that our brains love!

Why Three?

Think about it: "Ready, Set, Go!" ... "Lights, Camera, Action!" ... "Gold, Silver, Bronze!" Our brains LOVE threes.

  • Point 1: Your first reason or idea
  • Point 2: Your second reason or idea
  • Point 3: Your strongest reason or idea (save the best for last!)

Bridge Words (Transitions)

Transitions are the BRIDGES between your points. They help the audience follow along smoothly.

  • "First..." / "To begin with..."
  • "Next..." / "Also..." / "Another reason is..."
  • "Finally..." / "Most importantly..." / "Last but not least..."

Part 3: The Conclusion

10 Minutes
The Mic Drop Moment

Your conclusion is the LAST thing the audience hears. Make it count!

Never End With...

  • "So... yeah." (awkward silence)
  • "I'm done." (anticlimactic)
  • "That's it, I guess." (no confidence!)

The Two-Step Conclusion

A strong conclusion has TWO parts:

  • Step 1 - Summary: Remind the audience what you talked about. ("Today I told you about...")
  • Step 2 - Call to Action: Tell them what to DO with what they learned. ("So next time you... remember to...")

Example Conclusion

Topic: Why Dogs Are the Best Pets

Summary: "Today I shared three reasons why dogs are the best pets: they're loyal, they keep you active, and they always make you smile."

Call to Action: "So the next time you see a dog, give it a pat on the head - because that little guy might just become your best friend."

"End with a BANG, not a whisper. Leave your audience wanting more!"
