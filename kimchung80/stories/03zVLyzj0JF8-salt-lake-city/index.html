Christmas in Temple Square

We're glad this trip got mom into the holiday spirit. We got some gifts and this cool headband. Seasons greetings from Salt Lake City! Can't wait to come back home!!

~Gingy
Mom made us go to the museum which had the history of how they built the church. She said we had to learn something on this trip. Did you know they dragged rocks this big from 20 miles away to build the church??!? Mmmm hmmm. That's really far and heavy.
Hi everyone! It's me...Gingy! I'm in Salt Lake City. Mom came for work and I tagged along. I promise I'm still doing my homework and studying. But at night, Mom takes us out to see the sights!
Temple Square is where the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints has its headquarters. All of the buildings are white and very nice. Because it's Christmas they have lots of lights. And lots of people visit too. We were like everyone else trying to take pictures. Mmm hmmm!
This is the Assembly Hall. They have free concerts around Christmas at night. We went in and listened to some acapella.
There are some scenes from the Christmas stories. They had some figures that looked like Mom. We even saw a giraffe. I didn't know that giraffes hung out with cheetahs and zebras. Geoff would say that it's "So cool! So cool!"
Temple Square is across from a mall called City Creek. The mall is really pretty and has some nice shops. But Mom said it's not as good as Easton because there's no Jeni's.
The church looks so nice outside, I wanted to go inside. *knock knock* I guess no one is home.
I found a really nice gift for Mom but I don't have enough money. Maybe Dad can help out?
Chipper and I were tired so we took a break under the tree.
I don't want to be mean but Mom doesn't eat nice food. All she did was go to the food court at the mall. At least she did get this gingerbread latte. I don't know how I feel about that though. But Chipper is mad because she didn't get the praline chestnut latte which he says is a Southern delight.
