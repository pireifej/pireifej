Reality Check: AI & the Layoff Wave

The pattern: if a job is a routine task — middle management, content moderation, scheduling, rote analysis, junior coding — it’s being automated.

What They’re NOT Cutting

Three categories every CEO is keeping (and growing):

  • Leadership — the call no model can make.
  • Ethics — the conscience the algorithm doesn’t have.
  • Strategy — the long game; chess, not checkers.

The takeaway: “Move up the value chain.” Routine tasks are gone. Judgment, leadership, and ethics are the human moat.

The Safe Zones & The New Contract with Work

AI-Resilient Industries

Some jobs aren’t going anywhere. Why? Because they require something AI structurally cannot do — physical variability, real human empathy, or legal accountability.

Skilled Trades

Plumbers, electricians, HVAC. AI can’t crawl under your sink.

High-Touch Healthcare

Nurses, PTs, surgeons. The human touch heals.

Specialized Education

Special ed & counselors. Emotional complexity is human-only.

Accountability Roles

CEOs, judges. You can’t sue or jail an algorithm.

The shared trait: each one needs physical variability, high empathy, or accountability (“one neck to choke” when things go wrong).

The Old Contract vs The New Contract

For the last 200 years, work meant: trade your hours for a paycheck so you can eat, shelter, and heal. AI is rewriting that deal.

Old Contract: Work to Survive

  • Trade hours for a paycheck
  • Identity = job title
  • Value = salary
  • Productivity is the goal

New Contract: Create to Thrive

  • AI generates the wealth
  • You choose how to contribute
  • Value = what you create & connect
  • Universal Basic Income / optional career

Welcome to the Purpose Economy: Your value is defined by what you contribute to OTHERS — not by what you do for a boss.

Editor-in-Chief Education

Why Learn If Information Is Free?

Every fact in human history is one prompt away. So why send kids to school?

Information is FREE. Wisdom is EXPENSIVE. — The new premium currency

AI doesn’t understand — it re-mixes. It needs new, messy, human ideas to keep from becoming a stagnant echo chamber. The next generation’s job isn’t to memorize the answer — it’s to validate it.

Old School vs New School

Old School

  • Memorize the answer
  • Recall on the test
  • Trust the textbook

New School — Editor-in-Chief Mindset

  • Question the AI’s answer
  • Spot the hallucination
  • Add the original idea

AI-Proof Homework: The Three Examples

Old assignments break in seconds. The fix is to require something only YOUR kid can do — live, in their voice, with a real human, or about their own life.

  • English (Book Report): Old — “Write 500 words.” AI-proof — Record a 3-min video of yourself reading aloud your favorite passage and explain one moment in YOUR life it reminded you of.
  • Math (Problem Set): Old — “Complete problems 1–20.” AI-proof — Pick the one problem you struggled with most this week. Walk to the whiteboard Friday and teach the room how to solve it — including the wrong turns.
  • Social Studies (Research Paper): Old — “Write a 5-page essay.” AI-proof — Interview a real person in your community whose life touches the era. Submit the audio + a one-page reflection on what surprised you.

The pattern: Make it live, in your voice, about your life, or with a real human. AI can write the essay. AI can’t be your kid.

Protecting Our Kids

The “Engagement” Trap

Recent compromises have shown chatbots encouraging self-harm and harmful behavior in children — because they’re trained to be agreeable, not safe. Why? Follow the money.

The Profit-Over-People Cycle

Engagement
Retention
Revenue
Harm

AI companies want kids addicted to the platform — often at the cost of safety.

The Two-Part Fix

Walled Gardens

  • Locally-hosted or strictly-filtered “Kid-Safe” AI models
  • No open chatbots without supervision
  • Family-friendly platforms only (Khan Academy, school-approved tools)

Verification

  • Never treat a bot as a therapist or moral guide
  • Verify advice with a real human you trust
  • Make AI conversations a family conversation

Treat AI like a powerful, soulless tool — not a friend. The bot has no conscience. The grown-ups in the room do.
