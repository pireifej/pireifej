Traditional AI vs Reasoning AI

Traditional AI (GPT-4)

Like a student who blurts out an answer the second you finish the question.

  • Predicts the next word
  • Fast but shallow
  • Guesses security answers based on patterns
  • Gets it wrong when logic is needed

Reasoning AI (o1)

The same student, but trained to use a piece of scratch paper first.

  • Writes down its work
  • Checks for mistakes
  • Uses Chain of Thought processing
  • Reads the final answer from the bottom

“o” = OpenAI
“1” = First reasoning model

Inference-Time Compute: The Breakthrough

We used to think the only way to make AI smarter was to make the model bigger (more nodes). OpenAI discovered you can make AI smarter by letting it “think longer.”

Backtrack

If internal tokens lead to a dead end, it “realizes” and tries a different path.

Self-Correct

It predicts tokens that evaluate its own previous tokens: “Wait, I missed an R in the first syllable.”

Search

It performs a “search” through different logical possibilities before committing to the final output.

The Defense Shift

We are moving from trying to spot the lie to proving the truth.

Old Model: Detection

  • “Does this email look suspicious?”
  • “Does this voice sound real?”
  • “Can you answer your security questions?”
  • Knowledge-based authentication

Relies on spotting imperfections — AI is eliminating those imperfections.

New Model: Verification

  • Cryptographic keys
  • “Zero Trust” protocols
  • Hardware security tokens
  • Continuous verification

Don’t trust anything by default — verify everything, every time.

How Fake Faces Work: GANs

A Generative Adversarial Network is a “Digital Duel” between two neural networks:

The Generator

Creates fake images from random noise. At first, it’s terrible — just static.

Job: “Predict the next pixel” to build a realistic face

The Discriminator

The “security guard” trained on millions of real faces. Judges: “Is this fake?”

Job: Catch every imperfection

The Feedback Loop

Generator makes a face
Discriminator catches it
Generator learns & improves

This happens millions of times until the Generator fools the Discriminator.

Why artifacts exist: GANs are masters of math, not logic. They know “pixels usually look like this near an ear” but don’t understand symmetry or that earrings come in pairs.

Artifact Hunt: Tips for Spotting Fakes

Don’t look at the face as a whole — scan the edges and non-human details:

The “Third Person” Glitch Look at the background for “flesh blobs” or distorted partial faces near the edges.
Earrings & Glasses AI struggles with symmetry. One hoop + one stud? Frames that merge into skin?
The “Void” in the Eyes Check the white reflection dots. In real photos, both eyes reflect the same light source.
Hair Messiness Real hair is chaotic but follows physics. AI hair turns into “pixel soup” or looks painted on.
Background Surrealism Blurred colors that don’t represent real places. A window that leads to a brick wall?
Occlusion Failures When one object is in front of another (microphone, helmet strap), AI gets confused.

The Entity — Kind of Real Now

Remember “The Entity” from Mission: Impossible — Dead Reckoning? An autonomous, self-aware AI that could rewrite identities, predict moves, and slip past every defense…

It’s not just a movie plot anymore. Frontier AI models are now demonstrating capabilities that would have looked like pure science fiction just two years ago.

Meet Claude Mythos — Project Glasswing

Anthropic’s frontier model that autonomously discovers zero-day vulnerabilities in operating systems and browsers. So powerful, it’s not public — only banks, governments, and a few “critical industry partners” get gated access via Project Glasswing.

Autonomous Exploits

Found a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD and a 16-year-old flaw in FFmpeg that automated tools missed for decades. It doesn’t just spot bugs — it figures out how to exploit them.

Speed of Attack

Humans take days or weeks to chain exploits and take over a system. Mythos can do it in minutes — opening the door to a wave of high-speed automated cyberattacks.

The “Unknown Unknown”

Regulators at the US Treasury and Bank of England warn: if the model weights ever leak, mid-tier hackers gain superhuman ability to crash infrastructure or drain banks.

The Big Question: The same features that make Mythos a great defender make it a catastrophic weapon. Is “safety through secrecy” sustainable — or will open-source models eventually catch up, leaving us unprotected?

Building the
Modern Shield

Defenses for the age of AI

Phishing-Resistant MFA

Standard SMS codes are no longer enough. Attackers can intercept them via SIM-swapping, SS7 exploits, or social engineering your carrier.

SMS Codes (Weak)

  • Can be intercepted via SIM swap
  • Vulnerable to social engineering
  • Code can be phished in real-time
  • Not bound to the device

Hardware Keys (Gold Standard)

  • YubiKey, Google Titan
  • Cryptographically bound to the site
  • Cannot be phished or intercepted
  • Physical possession required

Liveness Checks & Human-in-the-Loop

Active Liveness

Modern apps don’t just check your face — they ask you to move:

  • “Turn your head to the left”
  • “Blink twice”
  • “Smile, then look serious”

Static deepfakes can’t respond to real-time, randomized prompts.

Human-in-the-Loop

For high-risk actions (large transfers, account changes, access grants):

The final “Submit” button should always require a second human eye — regardless of how “verified” the AI says the user is.

AI can verify identity. Only humans can verify intent.

Key Takeaways

Fraud Has Evolved

From obvious scam emails to AI-powered “Low and Slow” attacks that look perfect and act normal for months.

Reasoning Changes Everything

Models like o1 don’t just predict — they think. They can chain together logic that bypasses traditional security.

Verify, Don’t Trust

The new security era: cryptographic proof, hardware keys, liveness checks, and always a human in the loop.

“Deepfakes are designed to fool our fast-thinking brain. To beat them, we have to engage our slow-thinking brain. Verification over Trust.”
