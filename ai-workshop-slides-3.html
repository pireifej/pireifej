Stochastic
A fancy word for "random" or "probabilistic." Predicting the next word based on probability.
Parrot

How AI Sees Words

AI doesn't read words. It sees "tokens"—chunks of characters assigned to numbers.

unhappiness
un359
h71
appiness67391

Surprise!

These are real GPT-4 token IDs. Notice AI doesn't split words the way humans would—"un / h / appiness" instead of "un / happi / ness."

Why Those Numbers?

Token IDs are assigned by frequency. "un" is common (low ID: 359). "h" is very common (ID: 71). "appiness" is rare (high ID: 67,391).

Scale

GPT-4 has a vocabulary of ~100,000 tokens. Every response you've ever received is a sequence of predicted token numbers.

How It Actually Works

What happens from the moment you hit "Send" on a prompt?

1. Your Words Become Numbers

Your prompt gets converted into tokens (numbers). Think of it as a burst of electricity hitting the first layer of the neural network.

"What is the capital of France?"

2. The Weights React

Each node in the network has a weight (a multiplier). "Capital" boosts Geography and Government nodes. Cooking and Sports nodes stay quiet.

Geography ↑↑↑ Government ↑↑ Cooking ↓ Sports ↓

3. The "Attention" Filter

The AI uses Attention to decide which parts of your question matter most. It identifies that "Capital" and "France" are the strongest signals.

Capital + France → Paris

Why AI "Lies"

Since it only predicts the next word, it doesn't care about the truth. It cares about probability.

When It Works

"The capital of New Jersey is..." → Trenton (99% probability, happens to be correct)

When It Fails

Ask about something rare or fake, and it will still try to find the "most likely" words to complete the sentence—even if it has to make it up.

Key insight: AI hallucinations aren't "lies." The model doesn't know what's true. It only knows what words are statistically likely to follow other words.

Key Terms Recap

What is Responsible AI?
A safety manual for AI: fairness, transparency, accountability, and privacy. It's the brakes on the car—making sure AI doesn't cause harm.
What is Machine Learning?
A subset of AI where the computer learns from data instead of being explicitly programmed. A human says "look for wheels to identify a car."
What is Deep Learning?
Uses neural networks (signals, weights, nodes) so computers can handle complex data like photos, voices, and long paragraphs. The computer figures out on its own that wheels are a key feature of a car.
What is Generative AI?
AI that doesn't just analyze data—it creates new data. Discriminative says "that's a cat." Generative says "here's a new photo of a cat wearing a top hat."
What is a Hallucination?
When AI generates confident-sounding text that is factually wrong. It happens because the model predicts probable words, not truthful ones.

Q&A

Click any question to reveal the answer

Can AI ever truly "understand" language?
Current AI models don't understand language the way humans do. They recognize patterns and predict sequences. Whether "understanding" can emerge from enough pattern recognition is one of the biggest debates in AI research.
How do we stop hallucinations?
We can't completely stop them because they're built into how the model works—it predicts probable text, not truthful text. Solutions include retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), fact-checking layers, and teaching users to verify AI output.
Is AI actually creative?
AI can produce novel combinations of existing patterns, which looks creative. But it can't have intent, emotion, or lived experience. It's "creative" the way a kaleidoscope is creative—it rearranges existing pieces into new patterns.
