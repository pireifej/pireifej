Physical & Vocal Warm-Up

10 Minutes

The “Big Stretch”

Reach for the ceiling on tip-toes, stretching as tall as you can. Then drop and hang like a ragdoll, arms dangling loose. Shake it out!

The “Accordion” Breath

Put your hands on your ribs. Inhale for 4 counts (feel your ribs expand outward), hold for 4 counts, then exhale for 8 counts on a long “hisssss.” This deep rib-expansion breath powers your voice!

The Vocal Siren

Start at your lowest comfortable pitch on an “ooo” or “aaa” sound and slide smoothly to your highest note and back down, like a fire truck. Wheeee-ooooo! This stretches the vocal cords and increases range.

The “Volume Dial”

Pick a simple sentence: “Public speaking is a superpower.”

  • Say it at Volume 1 (whisper)
  • Say it at Volume 3 (conversational)
  • Say it at Volume 5 (speaking to the back of the room)

Practice “throwing” the sound to a specific point on the back wall without shouting.

The Anatomy of a Personal Story

10 Minutes
The Big Idea

A story isn’t just a list of things that happened — it needs a “heart.”

Plot Diagram

The Hook

Drop us into the action immediately. Don’t start with “So one day...” — start with a moment that grabs attention!

The Conflict

The “uh-oh” moment. What went wrong? What was scary, hard, or unexpected? This is the heart of every story.

The Resolution

How you fixed it — or what you learned. The audience should walk away with something meaningful.

Example: Hook vs. No Hook

Weak Start

“So, um, one day last summer my family went on vacation and we went to the beach and it was really fun...”

Strong Hook

“The wave hit me so hard I didn’t know which way was up. My feet couldn’t find the sand. And the only thing I could think was — I should have listened to my mom.”

“The audience doesn’t remember what you said. They remember how you made them FEEL.”

Crafting Exercise: The 3-Sentence Story

10 Minutes
The Challenge

Draft a tiny narrative in just 3 sentences to practice brevity and structure.

2

“But suddenly, I realized...”

“Because of that, I now know...”

Share Time

After drafting, volunteers stand up and share their 3-sentence story with the class using their Level 5 volume.

Audience feedback: “What was the heart of their story?”

Pro Tip

The best stories aren’t about the biggest events — they’re about the smallest moments that taught you something real.

Closing: The Hero’s Pose

5 Minutes

The Power Pose

Stand up tall. Feet shoulder-width apart. Hands on hips or fists raised above your head like a superhero. Hold for 30 seconds.

Research shows that “power posing” for just 30 seconds can make you feel more confident before you speak. End every session feeling like a hero!

“You are the hero of your own story. Stand like it. Speak like it. Own it.”

“1, 2, 3... HERO POSE!”

Homework Challenge

Practice your 3-sentence story at home using your “Level 5” volume. Say it to a family member, a pet, or even the mirror — but make sure the back wall can hear you!
